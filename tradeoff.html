How to read these results

Your ranking comes from numbers you estimated, so the real question isn't just "who wins?" — it's "how sure can I be?" These three views answer that. The golden rule: green / wide / high = trustworthy; red / narrow / low = fragile.

1

Weight sensitivity — "Does my winner survive if I weighted things differently?"

Each line is an option as you slide one criterion's importance from 0→100%. The bold ★ line is today's leader. If it stays on top across the whole width, your choice is robust to that criterion. Red markers show the exact weight where the leader flips — if one sits close to the "now" line, you're near a tipping point. Read the sentence below the chart for the plain-English version.

2

Decision robustness — "If all my numbers are a bit off, does the winner still win?"

The gauge shows how often the leader keeps 1st place across thousands of scenarios where every weight and grade is randomly nudged. Green (85%+) = trust it; amber = it usually holds; red = the result is a coin-flip. The bars show each option's share of wins. Widen the tolerance sliders to stress-test harder.

3

Critical weights — "Which single assumption is my decision riding on?"

Each bar reaches from a criterion's current weight to the point where the winner would change. Short red bar = fragile (a small change flips the result); long green bar = safe. Sorted most-fragile first, so the top row is the assumption worth double-checking. The label names who would take the lead.

4

Grade what-if — "What if one specific number is wrong?"

Pick an alternative and a criterion, then drag to set a hypothetical 1–5 rating. The lines show how every option's score reacts — for a cost criterion, a higher rating pushes that option down. Red markers flag the break-even rating where it changes rank, and the sentence below names who overtakes it. The heatmap above flags, for every grade, how many rating points of change it would take to flip the winner — redder = higher impact.
